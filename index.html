Harriet Tubman

The woman who freed a thousand slaves

Harriet Tubman, far left, with family and neighbors, circa 1887.

    Here is a timeline of Harriet Tubman's life:

  • 1820 - Harriet Tubman, born Araminta Ross, is born a slave on a plantation in Dorchester County, Maryland.
  • 1825 - Merely a child, Araminta is hired out to other households. Her first job is as a nursemaid where she is violently and frequently beaten when the baby cries.
  • 1833 - Araminta suffers a severe head injury from a metal weight aimed at a runaway slave. She begins having seizures which affect her for the rest of her life.
  • 1840 - Ben Ross, Araminta's father, is manumitted at the age of 45. She would learn that her mother's owner's will stipulated she and her children be manumitted also at age 45. The owners son would not honor his mother's will.
  • 1844 - Araminta marries a free, black man by the name of John Tubman.
  • 1849 - Araminta falls ill. The plantation owner dies leaving the plantation in a financial struggle. Three of Araminta's sisters are sold. Araminta escapes with her brothers. Her brothers have second thoughts and return to the plantation. With a $300 reward offered for her return, Araminta travels 90 miles to PA, using the underground railroad. She changes her name to Harriet in honor of her mother.
  • 1850 - The Fugitive Slave Law is passed. Harriet begins working with Thomas Garret and Frederick Douglas. Using her connections to the Underground Railroad, Harriet travels for the first time to help a family on their journey to fredom.
  • 1851 - She returns for her husband, but he refuses to leave. He chooses to stay with his new wife.
  • 1858 - After meeting John Brown, Tubman recruits support for the Harper's Ferry attack. Brown refers to her as General Tubman.
  • 1859 - Harper's Ferry Raid. John Brown is executed. Tubman purchases a piece of land on the outskirst of Auburn, New York. This would be her home the rest of her life.
  • 1860 - Harriet takes her last rescue mission in hopes of saving her sister, but discovers her sister has died. She saves another family instead. Lincoln is elected president.
  • 1861 - The American Civil War begins. Harriet works as a cook and a nurse in the south. She also helps former slaves for a regiment of African American soldiers and serves as a spy and scout for the Union.
  • 1863 - Harriet Tubman becomes the first woman to lead an assault during the Civil War. 700 slaves are set free.
  • 1865 - The Civil War ends and Tubman returns home.
  • 1869 - Harriet marries Nelson Davis, a man 22 years her junior. A biography of Tubman is published by Sarah Hopkins Bradford, titled Scenes in the Life of Harriet Tubman.
  • 1873 - She borrows money from a friend to buy gold and is attacked having the money stolen before she can make the exchange.
  • 1874 - Harriet and her husband adopt a baby girl.
  • 1880 - Her house in Auburn is destroyed by fire.
  • 1886 - Bradford publishes a second biography called Harriet, the Moses of her People.
  • 1898 - Tubman becomes involved in the women's suffrage movement and begins giving speeches.
  • 1903 - Harriet Tubman donates her property to a church in Auburn to be converted into a home for the aged and indigent people of color.
  • 1908 - The Harriet Tubman Home for the aged opens.
  • 1913 - Harriet Ross Tubman dies of pneumonia at the age of 93. She is burried with military honors in Auburn, New York.
